# Finova Mandatory Training & Compliance Policy

**Document reference:** FIN-HR-POLICY-TC-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective date:** 1 April 2026
**Review date:** 1 April 2027
**Policy owner:** HR Director
**Approved by:** Executive Committee

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out Finova's requirements for mandatory compliance training. It ensures that all
employees maintain the knowledge and skills necessary to meet regulatory obligations, protect
clients and the business, and uphold Finova's values.

Failure to complete mandatory training is a serious compliance matter. Finova operates in a
regulated financial services environment and is subject to oversight by the Financial Conduct
Authority (FCA). Adequate and demonstrable employee training is a regulatory requirement.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all Finova employees based in the United Kingdom and European Union,
including permanent employees, fixed-term contractors, and secondees.

Employees based in India (Gurgaon; Chennai, Pune, and Mumbai offices) are subject to a
separate training framework administered by Finova India and are not covered by this policy.

---

## 3. Required Courses

All employees within scope must complete all of the following mandatory courses:

1. **Bribery Prevention** — covering the Bribery Act 2010, Finova's zero-tolerance stance,
   red flags, reporting obligations, and hospitality & gifts policy.

2. **Data Protection** — covering UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018, personal data
   handling, data subject rights, breach reporting, and Finova's data protection obligations.

3. **DSE (Display Screen Equipment)** — covering workstation assessment, safe working
   practices, rest breaks, eye care, and reporting discomfort, in accordance with the Health
   and Safety (Display Screen Equipment) Regulations 1992.

4. **Fraud Prevention** — covering fraud typologies relevant to financial services,
   employee obligations under the Fraud Act 2006, internal fraud indicators, and reporting.

5. **Information Security** — covering cyber threats, password security, phishing
   recognition, clean desk policy, acceptable use of IT systems, and incident reporting.

6. **Responsible Use of Social Media** — covering Finova's social media policy,
   confidentiality obligations, reputational risk, and FCA-relevant communications guidance.

---

## 4. Completion Requirements

### 4.1 New Starters

All new employees must complete the full set of required courses within **90 days** of their
start date (the "new starter grace period"). New starters are exempt from overdue-compliance
status during this period.

Finova's learning platform (NovaLXP) will send new starters a welcome notification at the
start of their onboarding that lists all required courses and their completion deadline.

### 4.2 Annual Recertification

All employees must recertify on each required course within **12 months** of their most recent
completion. This applies to all in-scope employees, regardless of how long they have been
employed by Finova.

Recertification must be completed using the current version of each course available on
NovaLXP. Historical completions recorded on TalentLMS (Finova's previous learning platform,
active until February 2026) are recognised for the purposes of the 12-month window.

### 4.3 Reminder Notifications

Automated reminder emails are sent to employees as follows:

- **60 days before** a course completion expires: an initial renewal reminder.
- **30 days before** a course completion expires: a final renewal reminder.
- **Monthly** for all employees who are out of compliance: an overdue notice.

All reminder emails state the exact date by which training must be completed.

---

## 5. Non-Compliance

An employee is considered **non-compliant** if one or more required courses have not been
completed within the required timeframe (i.e., within the new starter grace period or within
12 months of the previous completion).

Non-compliance consequences:

- **First occurrence:** formal reminder issued; line manager notified by HR.
- **Ongoing non-compliance (>30 days overdue):** placed on Performance Improvement Plan
  for compliance; escalated to department head.
- **Persistent non-compliance (>60 days overdue):** formal disciplinary action, which may
  include termination of employment, consistent with Finova's Disciplinary Policy.

HR will maintain a monthly compliance status report and will share it with the Executive
Committee.

---

## 6. Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Employee** | Complete all required courses within the required timeframe; notify HR if there is a technical barrier to completion. |
| **Line Manager** | Ensure direct reports are aware of compliance obligations; escalate persistent non-compliance to HR. |
| **HR** | Maintain the compliance monitoring system; send notifications; maintain records; escalate non-compliance. |
| **Learning & Development** | Maintain course content in NovaLXP; update courses in response to regulatory changes; update the course list in this policy when required. |
| **HR Director (Policy Owner)** | Review and update this policy annually or when required by regulatory change. |

---

## 7. Records and Data Retention

Completion records are retained for a minimum of **7 years** from the date of completion.
Records are held in NovaLXP and in archived exports from TalentLMS.

---

## 8. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually. Material changes (such as additions to the required course
list or changes to grace periods) will be communicated to all employees and the compliance
monitoring system will be updated automatically upon upload of the revised policy document.
